Adding & Removing Group Members

First, the basics

You'll only see the groups you're allowed to manage. Each group is one card on the page. Two kinds of groups behave a little differently:

Microsoft 365 Group

Your change is submitted and applied in the background. It shows as Pending, then Completed — usually within a minute.

SharePoint Group

Your change applies immediately — no waiting.

The steps below are the same for both; the only difference is that a Microsoft 365 change may take a moment to show Completed.

Add a member

1

Open the group you want to change

Find the group's card and click its header to expand it.

2

Search for the person

In the “Search directory to add a member” box, type at least 3 letters of their name or email. Matching people appear below.

Click the person you want — their row highlights in blue.

3

Add a reason (if asked) and click Submit

If your page requires a reason, type it in the box. Then click the blue Submit button.

4

Done

The person now appears in Current members.

  • SharePoint Group — they're added right away.
  • Microsoft 365 Group — you'll see a “Request submitted” note and a Pending entry that turns Completed shortly (see the colors below). You can keep working while it processes.

Adding lots of people? Just repeat steps 2–3 for each. Microsoft 365 requests queue up and process one after another.

Remove a member

1

Find the person in Current members

Open the group (as in Add, step 1). Scroll the member list if needed, or use the filter box for big groups. Each person has a Remove button on the right.

2

Confirm in the pop-up

A confirmation window appears. Check the name and group, add a reason if asked, then click the red Remove button. (Click Cancel or press Esc to back out.)

The red button = the action really happens. Nothing is removed until you click it.

3

Done

The person drops out of Current members — immediately for a SharePoint group, or after a quick Pending → Completed for a Microsoft 365 group.

What the status colors mean

For Microsoft 365 groups, each change you make shows under “Your recent requests” with a colored label:

  • Pending — submitted, being processed. Normal for a few seconds.
  • Completed — done. The membership has changed.
  • Failed / Error — it didn't go through (e.g. it took too long, or something went wrong). Try again, or contact your administrator if it keeps happening.

Tips & troubleshooting

  • Type 3+ letters to search — one or two letters won't return results.
  • Big group? The member list scrolls, and a filter box appears so you can find a person fast.
  • Not sure it worked? Use the Refresh button on the group to re-check the live membership.
  • “No offices / no groups” showing? You may not be authorized for any groups on that page, or you're on the wrong page — check with your administrator.
  • Removing yourself or the last member? The confirmation will warn you first — read it before clicking the red Remove.
  • You can only add individual people to a Microsoft 365 group. (Adding a whole group is only available on SharePoint groups.)
